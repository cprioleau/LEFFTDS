FAQ

Review the questions and answers below to see if you find what you are looking for. If you don't, drop us a line.

Q: Is this study open now? How long does it run?

The study started in April 2015 and will run until April 2019. All sites are enrolling participants now.

Q: Who is eligble to join the study?

Participants must be between 18 and 90 years old. Healthy volunteers are accepted.

Inclusion Criteria:

  1. Member of family with a known mutation in one of the three major genes related to frontotemporal dementia: MAPT, PGRN, or C9ORF72
  2. Predominant type of dementia is cognitive/behavioral
  3. Reliable family member, friend, or caregiver who speaks with or sees the participant at least weekly
  4. Fluent in English
  5. Willing and able to undergo neuropsychological testing (at least at baseline visit)

Exclusion Criteria:

  1. Contraindication to magnetic resonance brain imaging
  2. Known presence of a structural brain lesion, such as a tumor or cortical infarct
  3. Presence of another neurological disorder that could impact the study results

Q: Will I receive a treatment for FTD?

Unfortunately no disease-modifying treatment extists yet for frontotemporal dementia. The goal of this study is to find markers that track the disease progression and can be used in clinical trials to see if new treatments work. You will receive medical attention through this study, and we will share any findings with your primary care physician.

Q: Will it cost me money to partipate?

There is no monetary cost to participate. The study will pay for medical costs related to study visits and will provide travel reimbursement in most cases.
